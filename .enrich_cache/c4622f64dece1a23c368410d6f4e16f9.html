Talks with Iran to resume this weekend in Pakistan: White House

Talks with Iran to resume this weekend in Pakistan: White House

Negotiators from the US and Iran may be getting ready for a fresh round of talks. The White House has confirmed it is sending a delegation back to Pakistan, and Iran's foreign minister is already in Islamabad.

Press Secretary Karoline Leavitt said the US side will include special envoy Steve Witkoff and President Donald Trump's son-in-law, Jared Kushner. They are set to travel on Saturday.

Leavitt said: "We've certainly seen some progress from the Iranian side in the last couple of days. Again, the president has made the decision to send Steve and Jared to hear the Iranians out. And so we'll see what they have to say this weekend."

She added that the meeting will be held at Tehran's request and that Vice President JD Vance is on "standby."

Iranian Foreign Minister Abbas Araghchi said he will be visiting Pakistan, Oman and Russia on a trip aimed at discussing "bilateral issues" and regional developments.

Mediators from Pakistan have been assisting the talks. Officials there say Araghchi and his team arrived on Friday. Sources say US security personnel are already on the ground.

However, Iran's Tasnim News Agency said no negotiations are planned and that Araghchi's trip is intended for talks with Pakistani officials.
