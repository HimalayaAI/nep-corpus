Energy Trade Strategy Prepared, Emphasis on Increasing Domestic Consumption

Energy Trade Strategy Prepared, Emphasis on Increasing Domestic Consumption

The government has prepared the “Energy Consumption Growth and Export Strategy, 2083” with the goal of completing 15 major hydropower projects within the next 10 years, significantly increasing domestic electricity consumption, and boosting electricity exports.

The strategy aims to generate 28,500 megawatts of electricity in Nepal by the year 2092, of which 13,500 megawatts will be consumed domestically and 15,000 megawatts will be exported.

A committee led by Joint Secretary Sandip Kumar Dev from the Ministry of Energy, Water Resources, and Irrigation submitted the 20-page strategy document to Energy Minister Biraj Bhakta Shrestha on Thursday. The high-level study committee was formed in line with point 74 (a) and (g) of the 100-point governance reform agenda approved by the Council of Ministers on Chaitra 13. The strategy aims to establish the energy sector as a key foundation for economic transformation.

Additional 24,500 Megawatts Production

To increase production, the strategy highlights that electricity will be generated within 10 years from 10 major projects, including:

  • 1,200 MW reservoir-based Budhi Gandaki
  • 1,061 MW Upper Arun
  • 670 MW Dudhkoshi

Within two years, it is estimated that more than 1,000 MW of solar electricity will be generated from projects promoted by the Nepal Electricity Authority. Over 10 years, including private sector solar projects, a total of 14,000 MW of electricity is expected.

24 High-Capacity Transmission Lines

To support this, 24 transmission lines, including cross-border ones, will be constructed. Expansion of internal distribution systems and substation capacity is also prioritized.

Within the current fiscal year, the 400 kV Hetauda–Dhalkebar section will be completed, and 14 transmission lines such as Dodhara Attariya–Lamki are expected to be ready by fiscal year 2090/91.

On the cross-border front, 400 kV lines including Dhalkebar–Muzaffarpur–Sitamarhi, Butwal, Gorakhpur, and Nanpara–Nepalgunj are planned for completion within the next fiscal year. The Chilime–Kerung cross-border transmission line to the north is targeted within 10 years.

Substation capacity is expected to reach 40,000 MVA within 10 years. The strategy also emphasizes improvements in internal distribution systems, installation of smart meters, and underground cabling.

Expansion of Energy Consumption

Currently, per capita energy consumption is about 450 kWh, which is targeted to reach 1,500 kWh within 10 years.

To increase domestic consumption:

  • Subsidies on cooking gas will gradually be removed and shifted to electric stoves
  • Promotion of energy-efficient electrical appliances in government offices, security agencies, hotels, and the service sector

The strategy also includes:

  • Prioritizing electric vehicles in public transportation
  • Expanding charging station networks
  • Converting coal and petroleum-based industrial boilers to electric systems

In agriculture, cluster-based “dedicated agriculture feeders” will be developed to increase energy use and productivity. Electricity tariff discounts will be offered to promote high-consumption industries such as fertilizer plants, data centers, and modern brick kilns.

Organized Export Strategy

The strategy aligns Nepal’s electricity exports with Indian demand, focusing particularly on peak evening hours to maximize economic returns. Reservoir and semi-reservoir projects will be operated in line with high-demand periods in the Indian market.

According to committee coordinator Sandip Kumar Dev, priority is also given to:

  • Increasing private sector participation in electricity trade
  • Structural reform of the Nepal Electricity Authority
  • Resolving legal complexities

The strategy identifies potential risks and proposes clear mitigation measures.

Additionally, it emphasizes expanding private sector involvement in electricity trade and improving efficiency through structural reforms in the Nepal Electricity Authority.

For effective implementation, a strict monitoring system will be adopted, and accountability will be ensured based on performance.

The study committee included officials from the Department of Electricity Development, Ministry of Energy, Electricity Regulatory Commission, and Nepal Electricity Authority, among others.
